Science

On my scientific interests

I am mainly interested in Computational Physics and also statistics. A natural interest is therefore Gravitational (Wave) Physics, especially the Data Analysis involved.

As a part of that, I wrote my Bachelor's thesis on "Systematic Differences in the Source Properties of the third Gravitational Wave Catalog" in Frank Ohme's group at the Albert-Einstein institute (AEI) in Hannover. In 2024, I returned to Frank's group at the AEI for my Master's thesis on "Systematic Errors in Gravitational Waveform Models". Many of the repositories on my GitHub came into existence during this time, along with the thesis itself that was submitted in May, 2025. One of the repos is a PyCBC plugin that was developed as part of the paper arXiv:2503.17400. Another one is the gw-signal-tools package, which provides useful tools to work with gravitational-wave signals and related data analysis tasks.
If you have time to spare, you can find and read the theses at DOI 10.15488/19414 and DOI 10.15488/19415. Code accompanying my Bachelor's thesis can be found here.

Currently, I continue to pursue my interest in gravitational-wave physics and data analysis during my PhD at Caltech.
